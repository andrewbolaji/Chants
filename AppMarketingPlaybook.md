# App Launch Playbook (under $500, one app at a time)

A repeatable, cheap, effective launch system for a new app from scratch, written for a solo founder with no marketing background. Run it start to finish on ONE app, then reuse it on the next. Do not run three at once.

The core truth: building the app is the easy 50%. Distribution is the other 50%, and it is the half that kills most indie apps. Budget your energy accordingly. Marketing is not a phase after launch; it starts before launch.

Hard budget: stay under $500 per app. Most of this playbook is free. The paid options are marked and ranked, so you spend only where it actually moves the needle.

---

## The one principle that governs everything

Go where your users already are. Do not try to make people come to you. Find the watering holes (the subreddits, Facebook groups, Discords, forums, hashtags) where your exact user already gathers and complains about the exact problem your app solves, and show up there with the solution. This is free, and it converts far better than ads, because the intent is already there.

Before anything else, write one sentence: "My app helps \[specific person\] do \[specific thing\] without \[specific pain\]." If you cannot fill that in sharply, no amount of marketing saves you. Everything below flows from that sentence.

---

## Phase 0: Foundations (before launch, mostly free, a few days)

1. **Lock the one-sentence pitch** (above). This becomes your app store subtitle, your landing page headline, your social bio, your first line in every post. Same sentence everywhere.  
     
2. **Landing page.** One page. Headline (the pitch), 3 screenshots or a 20-second screen recording, 3 bullet benefits (not features, benefits: "know who is in your house at 2am," not "check-in logging"), one clear button (Download, or Join the waitlist). Use a free builder (Carrd at ~~$19/yr is the cheapest good one, or a free Vercel/Netlify static page since you can build). Buy the domain if you have not (~~$12/yr). Add an email capture field even if the app is live, an email list is the one marketing asset you fully own.  
     
3. **One social home base, not five.** Pick the single platform where your user actually is, and go deep there instead of spreading thin. For a visual/consumer app, Instagram or TikTok. For a professional/niche tool, often X or LinkedIn, or a niche forum. Claim the handle everywhere for consistency, but only actively post on the one. You can add others once the first works.  
     
4. **A way to capture feedback and show life.** A simple email (hello@yourdomain) and the in-app suggestion box you already build. Responsive founders who reply fast turn first users into evangelists.  
     
5. **Press/launch kit, 30 minutes of prep that pays off all year.** A folder with: the logo, 3-5 clean screenshots, the one-sentence pitch, a 100-word description, and your founder story in 3 sentences (why you built it). You will paste from this constantly. Having it ready means you never scramble.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-launch (build a small audience before you ship)

The goal is to NOT launch to silence. Even 50 interested people make launch day feel alive and seed the algorithm.

1. **Build in public.** Post the journey on your one platform 2-3x a week: a screenshot, a problem you solved, a sneak peek, a "would you use this?" Real progress posts attract the exact people who care. This is free and it compounds. Your founder story (English-born Arsenal fan building a chant app; operator building the software his own house needed) is genuine and magnetic. Use it.  
     
2. **Collect waitlist emails** from the landing page. Even 30-100 is plenty for a first launch. These are your launch-day army.  
     
3. **Find your 5 watering holes.** List the 5 specific places your user gathers (exact subreddit names, Facebook group names, Discord servers, hashtags, forums). Join them now, weeks before launch. Be a real member: answer questions, be helpful, never spam. When you launch, you are a known member sharing a thing, not a stranger advertising. This single step beats most paid advertising for a niche app.  
     
4. **Line up 5-10 soft-launch users** who will try it early and give honest feedback (and ideally a testimonial or rating on day one). Friends, the waitlist, people from the watering holes who raised their hand.

---

## Phase 2: Launch (the spike, mostly free)

Launch is a moment you manufacture, not a thing that happens. Pick a date, tell the waitlist it is coming, then go loud across one concentrated window.

1. **Email the waitlist first**, the morning of. They are warmest. "It is live, here is the link, I would love your honest take, and a rating helps more than you know."  
     
2. **Post to your watering holes**, tailored to each, not copy-pasted. Lead with the problem and your story, not "check out my app." In a reentry-housing group: "I run a boarding home and built the tool I wished existed for inspections and check-ins, sharing in case it helps anyone here." In a football group: "made a thing for finding and creating chants, would love feedback from real supporters." Genuine, member-to-member, with the link.  
     
3. **Launch aggregators (free, high-intent traffic):**  
     
   - **Product Hunt:** the big one. Free. Launch on a Tuesday-Thursday, have your soft-launch users ready to upvote and comment early (the first hours decide everything). Prep your kit the night before.  
   - **Reddit** relevant subreddits (read each one's self-promo rules first, some have a dedicated day or thread).  
   - **Hacker News** Show HN, if it is a tool/tech angle.  
   - **Indie directories:** BetaList, there's a bunch, free listings.

   

4. **Post your founder story** as content, not an ad. People share stories, not features. "Why I built this" performs far better than "what it does."  
     
5. **Reply to everything** the first 48 hours. Every comment, every email, fast. Early responsiveness creates your first fans and your first word-of-mouth.

---

## Phase 3: Post-launch (the grind that actually builds it, free \+ selective paid)

The launch spike fades in days. The business is built in the months after, through consistency.

1. **Content cadence, 2-3 posts a week on your one platform, forever.** Short, useful, on-topic. For the chant app: clip a great chant, "chant of the week," a poll on which new signing needs one. For Roster: a tip for operators, a compliance reminder, a behind-the-scenes. Consistency beats brilliance. The algorithm rewards showing up.  
     
2. **Turn users into the marketing.** Make sharing native to the app (you already planned share-to-X/TikTok for the chant app, that IS the growth engine, lean on it). Ask happy users for app store ratings directly, ratings drive organic store discovery more than anything. A simple in-app "enjoying this? rate us" after a good moment.  
     
3. **SEO groundwork, free and compounding.** A few simple blog posts or help pages answering what your users search ("how to pass a boarding home inspection in Texas," "Arsenal chants lyrics"). Free, ranks over time, brings users for years. Pairs perfectly with your self-generating handbook, repurpose handbook sections into public help pages.  
     
4. **Keep showing up in the watering holes** as a helpful member, not just at launch. Ongoing presence is ongoing free distribution.

## Where to spend the money (ranked, stay under $500 total per app)

Spend only after the free channels show the app resonates. Paying to promote something nobody organically wants just buys you a faster no. In order of return:

1. **Domain \+ landing page: \~$30/yr.** Non-negotiable, basically free. Do this always.  
2. **A small batch of targeted ads ONLY to test, \~$50-100.** Once you have a converting landing page and proof people want it, a tiny Instagram/Facebook/Reddit ad spend, tightly targeted to your exact user, tells you your cost to acquire a user. Treat it as research, not growth. If $50 brings real signups, scale a little. If it brings nothing, stop, the product or message needs work, not more money.  
3. **One micro-influencer in your niche, \~$100-300.** Far better ROI than broad ads for a niche app. A football-culture creator with 10-50k engaged followers posting your chant app reaches more real buyers than $300 of scattershot ads. For Roster, a reentry or small-landlord newsletter/creator. One authentic post from a trusted niche voice beats everything else paid. This is where your best dollars go.  
4. **Skip for now:** expensive PR firms, broad untargeted ads, paid directories that are not high-intent, anything promising "10k downloads." None of it fits a $500 budget or a niche app.

Suggested allocation per app: \~$30 foundations, \~$100 ad test, \~$200 one micro-influencer, \~$170 held in reserve for whatever is working. You will likely not spend it all, and that is fine.

---

## The 10-step short version (pin this)

1. Write the one-sentence pitch. Use it everywhere.  
2. Build a one-page landing site with an email capture (\~$30/yr).  
3. Pick ONE social platform and go deep, not five shallow.  
4. Make a reusable launch kit (logo, screenshots, descriptions, story).  
5. Build in public 2-3x/week before launch; collect waitlist emails.  
6. Join the 5 places your users already gather; be a real member.  
7. Launch on a set date: email the list, post Product Hunt \+ your watering holes, lead with your story.  
8. Reply to everything for 48 hours. Ask for ratings.  
9. Post 2-3x/week forever; make in-app sharing easy; repurpose your handbook into SEO help pages.  
10. Spend selectively: $30 foundations, \~$100 ad test, \~$200 one niche micro-influencer. Stop paying anything that does not work.

---

## Founder note

Run this on ONE app at a time. The first launch teaches you the system; the second and third get easier and cheaper because you have the kit, the muscle, and the lessons. Three half-launches lose to one real one. Space them. Let the winner earn the next round of your attention.
